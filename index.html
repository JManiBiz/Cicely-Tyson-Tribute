Cicely Tyson

Influential African-American actress

Cicely Tyson with her award at the 67th Annual Tony Awards in 2013.

Cicely Tyson was an African-American actress known for her work in Sounder, Diary of a Mad Black Woman and, most recently, How to Get Away with Murder. She is revered for her refusal to play roles that demeans black women and for being an integral part of Tyler Perry films. She passed away in January 28th at the age of 96.

Here is a timeline of Cicely Tyson's life and achievements:

  • 1924: Born in East Harlem to Fredericka Tyson and William Augustine Tyson.

  • 1941: Had a daughter, Joan.

  • 1942: Got married to Kenneth Franklin.

  • 1951: Landed first acting role on the NBC television series Frontiers of Faith.

  • 1956: Played her first film role in Carib Gold. Her marriage to Kenneth Franklin was dissolved.

  • 1959: Had minor roles in several films including Odds Against Tomorrow and The Last Angry Man

  • 1958: First stge appearance in Dark of the Moon.

  • 1960s: Appeared in the original cast of The Black's. Won the 1961-1962 Vernon Rice Award for her performance in Moon on a Rainbow Shawl. Played a secretary in East Side/West Side in 1963-1964 where she was the only regular African-American of a TV cast at that time. Also had a recurring role in The Guiding Light. Had appearances in a Man Called Adam in 1966 and The Heart is a Lonely Hunter in 1968. She starred in the film version of The Comedians in 1967.

  • 1972: Played Rebecca Morgan in Sounder, one of her most recognized works. For this film, she was nominated for the Academny Award and the Golden Globe Award for Best Actress. She also won the NSFC Best Actress and NBR Best Actress awards.

  • 1974: Played the title role in The Autobiography of Miss Jane Pittman. This role won her a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress and an Emmy Award for Actress of the Year. She was also nominated for a BAFTA Award for Best Actress in a Leading Role.

  • 1977-1986: Nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress for her role in the miniseries Roots in 1977. Nominated for Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Lead Actress for her role in the miniseries King in 1978. In 1980, she recieved the American Academy of Achievement. Received an NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Actress and was nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstading Lead Actress for her role in The Marva Collins Story in 1981. Married Miles Davis in 1978. She was awarded the Women in Film Crystal Award in 1982. For her part in the 1986 film Samaritan: The Mitch Snyder Story, she received an NAACP Image Award for Outstanding Actress. She recieved a Candace Award for Distinguished Service from the Nationl Coalition of 100 Black Women in 1988.

  • 1989-1995: Divorced Miles Davis in 1989. During these years, she appeared in several television series such as The Women of Brewster Place in 1989, Fried Green Tomatoes in 1991, and Sweet Justice in 1994-1995.

  • 1997-2020: In 1997, she was inducted in the Black Filmmakers Hall of Fame and was given a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. Some of her other notable films include: oldest Living Confederate Widow Tells All (1994), Hoodium (1997), A Lesson Before Dying (1999), Diary of a Mad Black Woman (2005), and Because of winn-Dixie (2005). She made an appearance in Why Did I Get Married Too? in 2010 and narrated the Up from the Bottoms: The Search for the American Dream documentary. In 2011 she appeared in Willow Smith's music video for 21st Centary Girl and played Constantine Jefferson in the period drama The Help. In 2013, she won the Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play for her part in The Trip to Bountiful, won the Drama Desk Award for Outstanding Actress in a Play and the Oute Critics Circle Award for Outstanding Actress in a Play. She played a supporting role in The Haunting in Connecticut 2: Ghost of Georgia and in 2014 she had a reoccuring guest-star role in How to Get Away with Murder. This role nominated her for a Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Drama Series in 2015, 2017, 2018 and 2019. In 2020, she starred in A Fall from Grace.

  • 2021: She died at the age of 96.

Throughout her life, Cicely Tyson recieved several honours outside of the entertainment industry. In 2015, she received a Kennedy Center Honor. Then President Barack Obama bestowed on her the Presidential Medel of Freedom in 2016. In 2020 she was inducted in the Television Hall of Fame and receieved a Career Achievement Peabody Award. She received honourary degrees from Clark Atlanta University, Columbia University, Howard Universtiy and Morehouse College. The Cicely Tyson School of Performing and Fine Arts was named after her in 2009.
